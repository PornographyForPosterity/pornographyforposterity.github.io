### How entries are built

Each performer or studio entry starts from publicly available industry records — trade databases, contemporaneous press coverage, official studio credits, and, where available, the performer's own public statements about their own career. Entries record:

- **Names and aliases** a performer or studio worked under, since stage names changed frequently and the same person is often catalogued under several unconnected names elsewhere.
- **Studio and release information** — what was produced, by whom, and when.
- **Categories and tags** describing genre, format, and era, so the catalog is browsable rather than just a flat list.
- **Citations** back to the external records a fact was drawn from, so every entry is checkable rather than taken on faith.

Entries are deliberately minimal. PFP records the facts needed to place a person or release in the historical record — not commentary, ratings, or anything resembling a review.

### Correcting the record

Industry databases disagree with each other constantly — on spellings, dates, and even whether two names belong to the same person. Where sources conflict, PFP notes the discrepancy rather than picking one silently. If you find an error, see [Contribute](/contribute/).

### Scope & Ethics

**In scope:** documentation of adult performers and studios operating (or having operated) commercially and legally, built from public-record sources.

**Out of scope, permanently:**

- Any material involving minors, non-consensual acts, or content that is itself illegal to catalog or reference. This project documents that an industry existed; it does not launder or platform illegal content, and any such content is out of scope regardless of source.
- Private, non-public information about a performer that isn't relevant to their professional record — home address, legal name where a performer has kept it private, family details, and similar. PFP catalogs a public professional history, not a person's private life.
- Actual film or image files. See [About](/about/#what-this-project-is-not).

**Performer requests:** a performer (or verified representative) can request correction or removal of their entry. Historical accuracy matters, but so does the fact that these are real people, and PFP defaults to honoring such requests rather than arguing the historiography.

### A living document

This methodology will change as the project grows past its initial pre-digital-era focus — see [History & Scope Notes](/history/) for why that boundary was chosen and how it might move.
