Frequently Asked Questions - Sun, Jul 12, 2026

Common questions about what PFP catalogs, why, and how.

Isn’t this all already documented somewhere?

Partially. General-purpose industry databases exist and PFP relies on them as sources. What’s missing is a project treating this specifically as historical preservation rather than as a current, commercially-oriented directory — one concerned with what gets lost as studios close, sites go offline, and performers from earlier eras are forgotten, rather than only what’s current.

Why does this matter? It’s just pornography.

“Just” is doing a lot of work in that sentence. Pornography is a multi-billion-dollar media industry that has existed in roughly its modern commercial form for over half a century, employed a very large number of people, and been shaped by (and shaped) home video, cable, the internet, and payment technology in turn. Whatever one’s view of the material, the fact of the industry — who was in it, how it operated, how it changed — is a legitimate subject for historical record, the same way we keep records of other industries whose products a given reader might personally find distasteful.

Why start with the pre-digital era instead of everything?

Volume and risk. The pre-2000s-ish era of commercial pornography was produced by a countable number of studios on physical media, and its record is actively disappearing — box art, trade press, and physical releases don’t get archived by default the way digital content at least has a chance of being. The post-explosion era, with effectively uncountable user-generated and platform content, needs different tooling and priorities to catalog responsibly. Starting narrow and expanding is a deliberate choice, not a value judgment about which era matters more. See History & Scope Notes.

Do you host videos or images?

No. PFP is a metadata catalog — names, dates, studio credits, categories, and citations. It does not host, link to, or distribute the underlying films or photographs. See About.

How do you handle performers who want their information removed or corrected?

We honor removal and correction requests from performers or their verified representatives. See Methodology: Scope & Ethics.
